## Strategic Frame

1.8.2 has two strategic drivers:

1. **Ron's feedback (MAGI-minimization segment)**: The early-retiree, sub-$1M-portfolio, ACA-eligible user. Already addressed structurally in 1.8.1 (ACA Subsidy Bar, repayment messaging). 1.8.2 deepens this with the three-marker cascade and Roth IRA contributions clarity.

2. **Higher-earner segment (analyst critique 2026-05-12)**: The $1M+ portfolio, IRMAA-conscious, widow-penalty-aware user. The app already has most engine capabilities; 1.8.2 surfaces them better and adds the genuinely missing piece (0% LTCG bracket harvesting awareness).

**Both segments share the same engine.** No mode-switching, no separate paths. Just adaptive emphasis based on user phase (age + ACA enabled + portfolio scale).

The framework guiding 1.8.2 is the refined 6-row table from the analyst response:

| Phase | Age | Primary strategy | Cross-phase |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1a: Early retirement, ACA-eligible | 55-64, mid portfolio | MAGI minimization for ACA + IRMAA-free conversion runway | + 0% LTCG harvesting |
| 1b: Early retirement, higher income | 55-64, large portfolio | IRMAA-free conversion runway + RSU/NQDC unwind | + plan AROUND ACA |
| 2: Conversion window | 65-72 | Strategic Roth conversions filling bracket headroom | + IRMAA tier awareness, NIIT |
| 3: RMD management | 73+ | RMD management + QCDs | + manage AGI floor |
| Cross-phase: Widow planning | All | Joint-year conversion aggression | Highest single tax event for couples |
| Cross-phase: Heir planning | All | Convert at your bracket vs heirs' brackets | SECURE 10-year rule sets comparison |

## Per-Item Specifications

### L1 — IRMAA-free conversion runway callout (ages 60-62)

**Background:** Per the analyst's strongest tactical insight, ages 60-62 are the most valuable conversion years for high-earners because conversions in those years don't affect Medicare premiums (the 2-year IRMAA lookback starts at age 63 for Medicare-at-65 enrollment). The app's existing "Conversion Opportunity Window" banner mentions "X years before RMDs" but doesn't capture this IRMAA-specific advantage.

**Files:**
- Modify: `RetireSmartIRA/CalloutBannersHost.swift` (or wherever conversion banners render — was added in earlier V2.0 work, may be in 1.x equivalent)
- Modify: `RetireSmartIRA/DashboardView.swift` (banner rendering)

**Behavior:**
When user's age is 60, 61, or 62:
- Show a new banner: **"IRMAA-Free Conversion Window"**
- Body: *"You're in your most valuable Roth conversion years. Conversions made now don't affect your Medicare premiums later. Once you hit age 63, your MAGI starts determining your Medicare IRMAA tier two years later."*
- Style: Same urgency-yellow as existing conversion window banner; dismissible via existing `dismissedInsightKeys` pattern.

**Acceptance:**
- Banner renders for age 60-62 users
- Hidden for age <60 or age >62
- Tappable to show explainer
- Dismissable per existing pattern
- Tests: banner-visibility logic

**Effort:** 1 day

- **Headroom for tax-free LTCG harvesting**
- Warning: "Every $1 of Roth conversion reduces this headroom by $1. Consider whether harvesting taxable-account gains is more valuable."

**Acceptance:**

- Updates live as Roth conversion slider moves (headroom decreases)
- Tests: bracket-boundary correctness, headroom-computation, interaction with Roth slider

**Effort:** 2 days

### L3 — Heir-bracket comparison for inherited IRA conversions

**Background:** The strongest argument for aggressive Roth conversions for higher-earners is the SECURE Act 10-year drain rule applied to non-spouse heirs. A $400K inherited IRA drained at heir's peak earning years (likely 32%/35% bracket) is taxed dramatically higher than the original owner could pay now. The Heir Tax Rate pill on the assumptions bar lets users set this but doesn't surface the comparison.

**Files:**
- New: `RetireSmartIRA/HeirTaxComparisonCard.swift`
- Modify: `RetireSmartIRA/TaxPlanningView.swift` (place near Roth conversion section)

**Behavior:**
A new card under the Roth Conversions section that shows:

> **Convert now vs. heirs pay later**
>
> Converting $100K now at your 24% bracket: **$24,000** in tax.
>
> Same $100K inherited under SECURE 10-year drain to a child at their 32% peak earnings bracket: **$32,000** in their taxes.
>
> Net family benefit of converting now: **$8,000** per $100K (over their lifetime).

Recompute live as user adjusts Roth conversion slider. Show actual user's marginal rate vs. heir's bracket from the Heir Tax Rate pill.

**Acceptance:**
- Card visible when user is doing Roth conversions OR has substantial Trad balance
- Computation reflects user's current marginal rate (not just bracket)
- Reflects user's Heir Tax Rate pill setting
- Tests: comparison math correctness for various bracket scenarios

**Effort:** 2 days
