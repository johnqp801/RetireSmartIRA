## Tone notes for future reference

What we deliberately did NOT do:
- ❌ Did not request expedited review (saves limited expedite credits for real fires)
- ❌ Did not complain about wait time being unreasonable
- ❌ Did not suggest a business deadline or pressure
- ❌ Did not mention testers waiting

This established the polite-status-check template — useful for any future case where review latency exceeds the "upper-end of normal" 4-5 day window. The script:

> "Submitted [date]. In Waiting for Review for [N] days. The matching [other platform] build from the same upload was approved on [date], so the binary is clean. Wanted to make sure nothing on my end is blocking review and check whether you need additional information."

The "matching platform was approved" line is the key credibility move — it tells the reviewer "this isn't a complaint, this is a triage hint that something administrative is the bottleneck, not the binary."

## Most likely outcomes (priors)

1. **Best case (~30%):** Mac 1.8.4 moves into review within 24-48 hours of inquiry, possibly faster
2. **Most common (~50%):** Reply saying "Your app is currently in queue, please wait" — useful as confirmation that nothing's broken, just slow
3. **Less common (~15%):** Reviewer flags an issue that wasn't visible to you (rare for same-binary cross-platform but possible — e.g., Mac-specific Sandbox entitlement or metadata issue)
4. **Unlikely (~5%):** No response within 2 business days — would warrant a one-time bump or moving to a forum/social channel

## Follow-up rules

- **If approved within 24h of inquiry:** that's a useful data point — log it. Suggests the inquiry itself nudged it through.
- **If reply says "still in queue":** wait another 3-5 business days before any further contact. Don't be Bryan Jepson with Apple.
- **If reply requests info:** respond same day, attach whatever they need, log the exchange in this file.
- **If 2+ business days pass with no response:** one polite bump on the Contact Us form referencing the original Case ID.
