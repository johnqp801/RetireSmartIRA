## 2. New Jersey tax work (1.9) — DONE, reviewed, `b61ba23`

Three changes, TDD, verified to the penny against the official NJ-1040 Worksheet D chart:
1. **Phaseout fix** — exclusion now `min((pension+IRA)×tier%, chartMax)` (was `min(pension,$100k)×tier%`, which under-excluded pension > $100k MFJ in a band). `chartMax` = $100k MFJ/$75k single (≤$100k tier) / tier%×totalIncome (bands) / $0 (>$150k).
2. **Worksheet D — Other Retirement Income Exclusion** — `otherRetirementIncomeExclusion: Bool` flag (NJ-only). Unused exclusion (chartMax − pension excl) shelters other income (interest/dividends/cap gains), gated **total ≤ $150k AND earned ≤ $3,000** (earned = `.consulting`).
3. **NJ personal exemptions** — $1,000 + $1,000 senior (65+) per spouse, applied as a `postExemptionDeduction` AFTER the income-gated bands (so it can't shift band selection).

**Headline consequence:** the $50k-div + $100k-pension MFJ $150k example dropped **$125,000 → $112,500 taxable** (Worksheet D shelters $12,500 of dividends). This is exactly Bob's point, quantified.
**Latent follow-up (logged):** a future direct caller of `calculateStateTax(...)` that omits `postExemptionDeduction` would drop NJ exemptions; all current callers route through wrappers.

## 3. New York fix + multi-state audit

**NY $20k double-exemption BUG fixed (`41364ac`).** NY had `pensionExemption: .partial(20k)` AND `iraWithdrawalExemption: .partial(20k)` with no shared-cap → $20k applied to pension AND IRA separately (~$40k/person, ~$80k MFJ). NY Tax Law §612(c)(3-a) is ONE combined $20k per qualifying individual. Fix = `pensionAndIRAShareSingleCap: true`. **Limitation (documented):** combined-cap sums HOUSEHOLD pension+IRA, so a concentrated-income MFJ couple may still slightly over-exempt vs true per-spouse caps (deferred follow-up).

**Multi-state worksheet audit (CA/NY/PA), logged decision-log 2026-06-18:**
- **NY** gaps beyond the $20k fix: government/federal/military pension FULL exclusion (we cap at $20k); NYC/Yonkers local income tax (unmodeled). Defer.
- **CA** mostly correct (SS exempt, pension/IRA fully taxable, cap gains ordinary, HSA add-back). Gaps: CA itemized deductions, exemption credits (~$149/person), HSA earnings taxation. Defer.
- **PA** best-modeled (all retirement income exempt, SS exempt, Class-3 loss isolation, early-dist basis). Niche gaps only.
- **Cross-cutting:** out-of-state muni interest = known `TODO(v1.8.4)` ([DataManager.swift:473]) affecting all states.
- **Verified #6/#7:** `capitalGainsTreatment` flag is **dead code** (never read in a calc) → NJ/NY/etc. tax gains as ordinary, correct; cleanup candidate. Tax-exempt-interest reversal is the documented v1.8.4 TODO.
