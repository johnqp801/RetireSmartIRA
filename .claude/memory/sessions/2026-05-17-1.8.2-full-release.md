## Notable findings (institutional knowledge)

### State-tax conformity (Phase 1, R3 side-quest)

R3's review surfaced that `scenarioTotalAboveTheLineDeductions` reduces federal AGI but did NOT reduce state taxable income for any state. For 401(k)/HSA/FSA this was correct by design — users enter W-2 Box 1 wages, already net of those deferrals, with an HSA addback for non-conforming CA/NJ. But for Traditional IRA + the new R3 Other deductions, it was a latent gap — most states conform federally. Fixed via two new `StateTaxConfig` flags (`traditionalIRAContributionsTaxableForState`, `otherPreTaxDeductionsTaxableForState`), both defaulting `false` (state-deductible).

PA 401(k) was the **opposite-direction** bug: users enter Box 1 (already net of 401k), but PA doesn't allow 401(k) deduction. Tax research (PA DOR, NJ NJIT-5, Mass.gov directive, etc.) confirmed **PA is the only US state that taxes employee 401(k) elective deferrals as of TY2026**. Fixed with a new `pretax401kContributionsTaxableForState` flag, true only for PA. Edge cases documented inline (NJ 403b/457/IRA non-conformity is out of app scope; MA solo-401(k) for sole proprietors is also out of scope; PA local EIT noted).

### OBBBA senior bonus phaseout (Phase 3 H4 — second real correctness fix)

The engine had OBBBA math but with a Tier-1 correctness bug: applied 6%-of-MAGI-above-threshold phaseout reduction **once to the combined $12K MFJ base**. Primary-source verification (IRC § 151(d)(5) statutory text quoted via CRS R48613 + IRS Newsroom OBBBA factsheet) confirmed the statute operates phaseout on **"the $6,000 amount" per qualifying individual**, then sums across qualifying individuals on the return.

Fix in commit `1d2d733`:
- Engine math changed in both `standardDeductionAmount` and the new named `seniorBonusDeductionAmount` accessor
- 4 dependent dollar values in `RetireSmartIRATests.swift` Scenario B (MFJ both 73, NY, MAGI $187,883) recalculated
- Added discriminating test at $220K MFJ-both-65 expecting $3,600 (would be $7,800 under the wrong math) to lock in the per-person structure

Impact: MFJ-both-65 households in $150K–$250K MAGI band were over-deducting up to $3K. Roth-conversion optimizer recommendations in that band were biased toward larger conversions than truly optimal.
