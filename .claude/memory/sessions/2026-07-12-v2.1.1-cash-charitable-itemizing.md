## IMPORTANT FINDING — the over-conversion "brake" never prevented full drain
`RealismRegressionTests.brakeStopsDrain()` failed when wired. Investigation (scratch harness, since deleted):
- At **λ=0** (owner-only objective) the optimizer **fully drains the $1.5M traditional to zero in EVERY regime** tested — states TX/CA/FL/NY, taxable liquidity $0/$50k/$200k/$500k/$1M, and a realistic SS+$40k-pension+$60k-expense case. termTrad=0 everywhere.
- The C3 gross-up + PV-discount "brake" (2026-06-26 realism batch) reduces the *rate* of over-conversion but never *prevents* full drain.
- `brakeStopsDrain` passed **only incidentally**: it used CA, whose **non-deductible** (pre-2.1.1) state income tax added just enough cost to hold termTrad marginally >0 in that one config. In the no-income-tax states (where 2.1.1 is a provable no-op) the IRA already fully drained pre-change.
- 2.1.1 correctly makes state income tax a deductible SALT itemizable → CA joined every other state at termTrad=0. **The feature did not break the brake; it un-masked a pre-existing limitation.** Reviewer (opus) independently excluded all bug modes (SALT is capped, no double-deduct, correct §68 AGI).

**Resolution (John, 2026-07-12): ship the feature; shelve the guard + follow-up.** `brakeStopsDrain` is `.disabled(...)` with a full reason (not deleted, not flipped green) — a live re-enable-when-fixed marker. The genuine product question is filed as a follow-up.

## Tracked Minor findings (for the final whole-branch review to triage)
- **AGI-basis seam (gross vs net) — SALT cap AND medical floor** (final-review finding): single-year is itself internally inconsistent — its charitable ceiling/floor and §68 use NET (`federalAGI.value`), but its **medical floor** (`estimatedAGI`/`scenarioGrossIncome`) and **SALT-cap phaseout** (`scenarioGrossIncome`) use GROSS. The T3 helper uses NET (`federalAGI`) for all four. Diverges only when above-the-line deductions (401k/HSA/trad-IRA) are nonzero AND the household itemizes medical/SALT (SALT-cap piece also needs MAGI > ~$500k). Non-biting for retirees (above-the-line=0); the helper's NET choice is arguably *more* correct than single-year's GROSS. **Parity test blind spot:** `MultiYearItemizedParityTests` uses pension-only income (above-the-line=0), so it can never detect this seam. Follow-up: (a) decide whether to thread a separate `magi`(gross) param for the medical-floor/SALT-cap calls to byte-match single-year, and (b) add a parity case with a nonzero above-the-line deduction so the guard can see the region. Low priority (working-household edge, out of Tier-2 retiree scope). Header note added at `MultiYearItemizedDeduction.swift`.
- **`.saltTax` local/city income tax dropped** from the multi-year projection entirely (engine recomputes STATE income tax per year, has no local line). Narrow, documented.
- **T6 Step-7 gross-up** holds the chosen deduction fixed as `dW` raises AGI (doesn't re-run std-vs-itemized during gross-up) — consistent with prior standard-only behavior, a minor approximation.
