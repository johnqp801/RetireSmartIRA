# HumbleDollar reply — Roth conversion "withholding loophole" (2026-07-11)

**Context:** A HumbleDollar poster asked for a 4th-quarter Roth-conversion strategy that treats the tax as withholding (for even-across-the-year credit) and funds the tax from a brokerage account. Gemini gave them a 3-step plan: (1) convert Traditional → Roth at 0% withholding; (2) take a separate Traditional-IRA distribution 100% withheld to the IRS; (3) 60-day rollover of brokerage cash into the **Roth** to "backfill" the distributed amount. They asked whether HD's tax gurus see any fatal flaws.

**Fatal flaw identified:** Step 3 routes the make-whole cash into the Roth, which makes it a second, fully taxable Roth conversion. The tax money becomes extra conversion equal to the tax, so a $100k conversion is actually $130k of taxable income, overshooting whatever bracket/IRMAA/NIIT target the conversion was sized to hit. (Engine check, MFJ 2026, $60k pension + $48k SS, both 68: the extra $30k adds ~$6,600 federal tax and crosses IRMAA tier 0 → tier 1.) The fix is to roll the make-whole cash back into the **Traditional** IRA (tax-free wash), not the Roth. Also: their one-rollover-per-year warning is misapplied (conversions are exempt); RMD-age dollars can't be converted/rolled over.

## Final posted reply (approved)

> If I understand your process correctly, my observation would be: The Step 2 distribution came out of a Traditional IRA. Roll it back into a Traditional within 60 days with brokerage cash and it's a tax-free wash. Roll it into the Roth instead and it's a Roth conversion, fully taxable, because you've moved Traditional money into a Roth.
>
> So the "tax money" doesn't pay the tax. It becomes an extra conversion equal to the tax, which means a $100,000 conversion you thought you did is actually $130,000.
>
> And that overshoot is the whole risk: if you sized the $100k to stay under a bracket or IRMAA threshold, the real $130k blows through it.

**Held in reserve** (only if the poster asks "so how should I do it?"): simplest clean path is convert at 0% withholding + pay the tax as a Q4 estimated payment from the brokerage + file Form 2210 Schedule AI (a Q4 conversion is Q4 income, annualizing removes the earlier-quarter penalty). If they insist on withholding treatment + outside funding, the Traditional-backfill version works but uses the single 60-day IRA rollover per 12 months.
