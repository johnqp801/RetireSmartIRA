Hi Alan,

Quick follow-up on the notes you sent. All three landed in version 2.1.2, which I'm submitting to Apple now. Here's where each one went:

- **Local city taxes.** There's now a "Local / city income tax" field in My Profile, right below your state. Enter NYC's 3.88% (or any locality's rate) and it's applied to your state-taxable income everywhere: the current-year numbers, the multi-year projection, all of it.

- **State withholding by percent.** State withholding now has the same $ / % toggle that federal got last release. Pick percent, enter the rate, done.

- **Numbers for 2027 and beyond.** Two parts to this one. First, the Multi-Year Plan tab already projects your income, taxes, and RMDs for every year out to your planning horizon, so the forward view you described is there today and worth a look if you haven't dug into that tab. Second, new in 2.1.2: you can now set your own expense numbers for any future year. Every year in the plan has an edit button; you can enter a one-time expense (say, a roof in 2028) or a lasting change to your annual spending, and the whole plan recalculates around it. Entering your own future income the same way is next on my list, and your note is a big part of why.

Beyond your items, 2.1.2 shows comparisons in today's dollars by default (so big future numbers don't distort the picture), recalculates instantly when you change any assumption, and includes a refined conversion engine that produces smoother year-by-year recommendations.

It should clear Apple's review in a day or two, so watch for the update in the App Store shortly.

Thanks again for the specifics. Notes like "percent works for federal, not state" are exactly the kind I can turn around fast, and telling your colleagues about the app means a lot.
