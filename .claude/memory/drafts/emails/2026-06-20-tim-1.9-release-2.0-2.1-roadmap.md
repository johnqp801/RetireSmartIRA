Hi Tim,

Good news, the feature you pushed me on is built and submitted. Version 1.9 adds a Retirement Drawdown view in the RMD Calculator that projects how your IRA and 401(k) balances draw down year by year, out to 40 years, three ways:

- RMDs only, your baseline if you don't voluntarily touch the accounts
- A target spending level, where guaranteed income (Social Security and pensions) is counted first and only the gap is pulled from the accounts, the way you described it
- A withdrawal rate, for the 4% to 4.7% rule

It includes an inflation input, a today's-dollars toggle, and an indicator for when your projected income would reach the first Medicare IRMAA tier. It's in Apple's hands now, and I expect review and approval to the App Store early this week.

That covers the core of what you raised, modeling the drawdown instead of just watching the balances grow. The two pieces that go further are exactly where I'm headed next, and I'm working on them now:

2.0 (next): tax-aware multi-year planning. This ties the drawdown to the year-by-year tax picture and adds Roth conversion optimization across your bridge years, sized to a goal you choose, smoothing brackets or minimizing lifetime tax, not just maximizing the ending balance. That is the conversion-ladder optimization you described, with the objective being yours to set.

2.1 (after that): brokerage accounts and withdrawal order. This adds a taxable brokerage account with your long-term capital gains treatment, and models the two orderings you laid out, pro-rata across accounts or sequential (brokerage first, then IRAs, Roth last), so you can see which sequence keeps lifetime tax and IRMAA down.

Your input shaped 1.9 directly, and it's steering 2.0 and 2.1. I'll keep you posted as each comes together, and I'd welcome your read on the drawdown view once it's live.

Best,
John
