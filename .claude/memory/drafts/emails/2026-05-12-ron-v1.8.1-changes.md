# Email to Ron — V1.8.1 changes from his beta feedback

**Date sent:** 2026-05-12 (approximate)
**Recipient:** Ron Park
**Context:** Ron is a sub-$1M MFJ retiree, ACA-focused beta tester. He gave detailed feedback on V1.8.1 build 36 on 2026-05-11. This email accompanies the updated DMG / TestFlight invite for build 37.

---

Subject: RetireSmartIRA 1.8.1 build 37 — your feedback, applied

Hi Ron,

Thanks again for the thorough notes on the last build. Here's what changed in this one — every item below came directly from your feedback.

**Correctness fixes**

1. **HSA contribution limit** — Updated to the 2026 IRS values (Rev. Proc. 2025-19): $4,400 self-only / $8,750 family / $1,000 catch-up at 55+. The stale number you spotted is gone.

2. **ACA cliff threshold** — Was showing ~$82K for a household of 2; now correctly shows $84,600 (400% of the 2026 HHS poverty guideline of $21,150). Fixed for all household sizes, plus Alaska/Hawaii multipliers.

3. **"Total Taxable" header now responds to pre-tax contributions** — You caught that Roth conversions updated the header but 401(k)/Traditional IRA/HSA contributions didn't. They do now, and the federal tax number flows correctly from there.

4. **Silver plan row duplicate** — The two $650 values on the Settings row are gone. There's now a single, clearly-labeled "Silver plan (monthly premium)" field.

5. **Missing #4 in the Scenarios numbered list** — Step numbers are now computed dynamically from visible sections, so you'll never see 1-2-3-5 again regardless of which sections are gated off for your profile.

**UX changes from your suggestions**

6. **Scenarios sections reordered** to tell a coherent strategic story:
   1. Pre-tax Contributions (reduce AGI first)
   2. Charitable Contributions
   3. IRA/401(k) Withdrawals
   4. Roth Conversions

   This is the natural flow whether you're a MAGI-minimizer (your case) or a Roth-maximizer.

7. **"Extra Withdrawal" renamed to "Additional Withdrawal"** — Your point that "extra" implies "more than needed" was right. "Additional" is neutral.

8. **Full accounting disclosure on Scenarios** — A new "How is Total Taxable computed?" expandable section right below the header. Shows Income − Deduction − Pre-Tax Contributions + Roth Conversions = Taxable, with sign-coded numbers.

9. **Silver plan caveat + KFF link** — Below the Silver plan input, there's now a caption explaining that $650/month is the national-average benchmark for a family of 2 and that actual premiums depend on state/age/income, plus a direct link to KFF's calculator (which works in all 50 states, unlike healthcare.gov which forwards CA users to the often-broken CoveredCA flow).

10. **ACA cliff messaging now emphasizes REPAYMENT** — This was your most important catch. Previous language said the cliff "costs $17K/yr in lost subsidy," which understates the damage. New language makes clear that if you've taken advance premium credits during the year and then cross the cliff, you have to **repay** those credits at tax time — a tax-bill surprise, not just a future-year subsidy loss. Under post-IRA 2022 rules (cliff returns in 2026), there's no repayment cap, so the framing matters a lot.

11. **Deductions section forward-pointer** — There's now a tip card in the Income/Deductions area pointing you to Scenarios for the pre-tax contributions interaction, since that's where you can see them affect Total Taxable in real time.

**What's the same**

Version is still 1.8.1; this is build 37 (last one you saw was build 36). All your prior preferences and any scenarios you saved will carry over — no data migration needed.

If anything still feels off, please flag it. I'm planning to submit 1.8.1 to the App Store today, but a follow-up 1.8.2 is already scoped and there's room to roll your next round of notes into it.

Thanks,
John
